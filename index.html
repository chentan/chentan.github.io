I received my Ph.D. in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering from the University of Notre Dame under the supervision of Prof. Bill Goodwine. Prior to that, I received my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), China, and dual Master's degrees from SJTU and Ecole des Mines de Douai, France. My research interests are in dynamics, nonlinear control theory and their applications to robots, such as legged robots and manipulators. My plan is to merge nonlinear geometric control approaches with emerging optimization and learning tools to develop robust and safe controllers for the robots. My current interests of robotic applications lie in health care and smart manufacturing.

Currently I am a postdoc associate working with Profs. Timothy Bretl, Katherine Driggs-Campbell and Nancy Amato on an industry-funded research project on smart manufacturing.
